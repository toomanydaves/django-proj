My name is Sean Jacke. [3]
I'm the prime mover behind Too Many Daves; the one who started things in motion.
In what follows, I'm gonna do my best to bring you up to speed.

Cologne, Germany
Fall 2017

"Too Many Daves" has its origins almost twenty years ago and half a world away at Habibi's Hutch, a preschool in Austin, Texas where I first encountered the poem. It didn't take more than a couple times reading it aloud and the sound of the kids' laughter for me to recognize something special in it.

As I'd follow along my path, it would stay with me, providing inspiration along the way.

School in the Cloud

I was already living in Europe when I learned how to code professionally. I wanted to focus on educational software. The thoughtful and creative integration of 21st century technology has boundless potential to democratize and enliven educational opportunities.

Lebenskunst

Back in Austin, and previously and afterwards in New York, I had been a student myself, as well as a teacher. In my spare time, I'd learned how to draft, weld, solder glass and work with wood – I'd begun making things in earnest.

In the intervening years, I've met many fellow artists, makers and designers. And over time I realized that there was something I needed to create for myself and these like-minded folks – a platform where works of art can be curated and past on to others in a way both sustainable for the artists, designers and makers, as well as affordable for everyday folks looking for authentic goods.

You Better

After the birth of my two sons, I started learning even more about the person I want to be and what I want to achieve. I decided to use my programming skills to begin development on an app to help me, and whoever else, set and reach personal goals. To let it shine!

Program or Be Programmed[4]

These experiences define who I am, form the core projects in the current portfolio and reflect the spirit of Too Many Daves:

  • School in the Cloud
  • Lebenskunst
  • You Better

And they are already in very good company!

They are surrounded by many other similarly focused, innovative projects. Some in collaboration. Some just known to Too Many Daves as worthy of admiration. All in various stages of realization. All, I'd submit, worthy of your attention.

Lay of the Land

As a network, Too Many Daves itself is in its infancy. In fact it's literally just crossed that most crucial threshold of existence – it's come into the world – or as the case may be, the World Wide Web. What was even recently just a construct, a vision in my mind, finally has a form to exist on its own, and a way to make itself known.

Heck, it has you reading about it!

And should you be as interested in what you see as I hope you will be, there are plenty of opportunities to contribute a verse[2]. Even though it is still very much my baby, Too Many Daves was always meant to exist apart from me, with plenty of room to accomodate anyone who wants to stand for self-expression, creativity, tolerance and wonder.

Thanks very much for hearing me out.
If I don't already, I hope to get to know you very soon!

Sean Jacke
Founder, Too Many Daves
